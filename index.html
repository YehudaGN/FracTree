Description

×

This program is a visualization of fractal trees. A fractal is an endless simple pattern repeating itself. These trees are generated using a recursive function which continuously generates branches until the length of the branches reaches a small enough size. At this point, a function is called to generate leaves on the very last branch.

There are over 52 billion variations of trees that can generate, not including any colors. When you include colors it gets well over the undecillions... and then I stopped counting.

I hope you enjoy playing around with this program and seeing what kind of trees you can generate, random or not.

Instructions

×

You can adjust the sliders on the right side of the screen to affect how the tree generates.

There are a lot of different variables that get affected so you can generate wildly different types of trees. I encourage you to mess around and see what you can create.

You can also choose what color you would like the tree to be as well as choose the color of the leaves.

You can adjust the color and glow strength or even remove completely, the glow affect that generates alongside the tree.

When you are finished with your adjustments, click the "Generate Tree" button.

Alternatively, you can generate a random tree by clicking the "Generate Random Tree" button.

(The Branch Shrink % can take a long time to run and may even crash your browser as the program works recursively and this extends how many recursive calls are made)
